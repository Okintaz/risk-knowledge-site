Risk Management Frameworks

Risk management frameworks provide structured guidance for identifying, assessing, and managing risks in a consistent and repeatable manner. One of the most widely adopted frameworks is ISO 31000.

ISO 31000

An international standard that outlines principles, a framework, and a process for managing risk across organizations.

Enterprise Risk Management (ERM)

A holistic approach that considers strategic, financial, operational, and compliance risks collectively.

Risk Appetite & Tolerance

Defines how much risk an organization is willing to accept in pursuit of its objectives.

Why Risk Management Matters

Informed Decision-Making

Risk management enables leaders to evaluate uncertainty and make decisions with a clearer understanding of potential outcomes.

Governance & Accountability

It supports strong governance by clarifying risk ownership, controls, and reporting responsibilities.

Resilience & Sustainability

Organizations with mature risk practices are better prepared to respond to disruption and long-term challenges.
