Risk Management Tools

Risk management tools translate uncertainty into structured, actionable information. These tools support organizations in identifying, assessing, prioritizing, monitoring, and responding to risks across different functions and industries.

1. Risk Register

A risk register is the central record of an organization’s risk profile. It captures identified risks, their causes and impacts, assessment results, ownership, and mitigation actions.

Importantly, the risk register distinguishes between inherent risk (before controls) and residual risk (after controls are applied).

The risk register is a living document and should be reviewed regularly to reflect changes in internal conditions, external environment, and control effectiveness.

2. Risk Matrix

A risk matrix is used to visualize and prioritize risks based on their likelihood and impact. It supports consistent risk evaluation and decision-making.

Risk matrices are typically applied to both inherent risk and residual risk to assess the effectiveness of controls.

3. Key Risk Indicators (KRI)

Key Risk Indicators (KRIs) are measurable metrics that provide early warning signals of increasing risk exposure.

Risk Area KRI Threshold Current Value Status Management Response
Financial Liquidity coverage ratio < 110% 125% Normal Routine monitoring
Operational Critical system downtime (hours) > 2 hours 3 hours Warning Incident escalation
Compliance Outstanding audit findings > 5 7 Critical Immediate remediation

Effective KRIs focus on trends and thresholds rather than historical outcomes, enabling proactive risk management.
